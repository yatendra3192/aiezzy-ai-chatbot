How to Generate Perfect AI Images

Master these techniques to get professional results every time

1

Writing Effective Prompts

Start with the subject: Begin your prompt with the main subject ("a mountain landscape", "a professional headshot", "a fantasy dragon"). Be specific about what you want to see.

Add descriptive details: Include adjectives that describe style, mood, and atmosphere ("photorealistic", "watercolor painting", "cyberpunk aesthetic", "warm lighting", "dramatic composition").

Specify technical aspects: Mention camera angles ("aerial view", "close-up portrait", "wide angle"), lighting conditions ("golden hour", "studio lighting", "neon glow"), and quality indicators ("8K resolution", "highly detailed", "professional photography").

Example transformation: "cat" ‚Üí "a fluffy orange tabby cat with green eyes, sitting on a windowsill, soft natural lighting, photorealistic, highly detailed fur texture, bokeh background"

2

Choosing the Right Style

Photorealistic: Use terms like "photorealistic", "DSLR photography", "high resolution", "professional photo" for images that look like real photographs. Perfect for business use, marketing materials, and realistic scenes.

Artistic styles: Reference specific art movements ("impressionist", "art nouveau", "surrealist") or artists' styles. Terms like "oil painting", "watercolor", "digital art", "concept art" guide the aesthetic direction.

Illustration styles: Specify "cartoon", "anime", "comic book", "vector art", "children's book illustration" for non-photorealistic images. These work well for educational content, entertainment, and youth-oriented materials.

Mixed media: Combine styles for unique results: "photorealistic portrait with watercolor background" or "3D render with hand-painted textures" creates distinctive hybrid aesthetics.

3

Iterating and Refining

Generate multiple variations: Create 3-5 versions with slightly different prompts to see which approach works best. Change one element at a time to understand how different terms affect results.

Refine based on results: If your first image is close but not perfect, identify what needs adjustment. Too dark? Add "bright lighting" or "well-lit". Wrong perspective? Specify "bird's eye view" or "eye level perspective".

Use negative prompts mentally: If you're getting unwanted elements, be more specific about what you DO want rather than focusing on exclusions. Instead of avoiding clutter, specify "minimalist composition" or "clean background".

Build on successful images: Once you find a prompt formula that works, save it as a template. Modify successful prompts for related images to maintain consistency across projects.

4

Post-Generation Enhancement

AI image editing: Use AIezzy's built-in image editor to make adjustments after generation. Change colors, add or remove elements, adjust composition, or modify specific details without regenerating entirely.

Multi-image fusion: Combine multiple AI-generated images to create composite scenes. Generate a background separately from foreground subjects, then merge them for complex compositions that would be difficult to prompt in one generation.

Image-to-video: Animate your generated images using AIezzy's image-to-video feature to create dynamic content for social media, presentations, or video projects.

Traditional editing tools: Download generated images and enhance them further with traditional photo editing software for final touches like color grading, sharpening, or text overlays.

Tips for Best Results

Expert recommendations to maximize your AI image generation success

Be Specific and Descriptive

The more details you provide, the better your results. Instead of "a house," try "a modern two-story house with large windows, wooden deck, surrounded by pine trees, sunset lighting, architectural photography style." Specific prompts give the AI clear direction and reduce ambiguity, resulting in images that match your vision more accurately.

2

Reference Art Styles and Movements

Mentioning specific art styles, movements, or even artist names (for style reference, not copying) dramatically improves results. Terms like "impressionist style," "cyberpunk aesthetic," "art deco design," or "Studio Ghibli inspired" provide strong stylistic guidance. Research art history and visual styles to build your prompt vocabulary and achieve more sophisticated results.

3

Use Quality and Technical Modifiers

Include quality indicators in your prompts to push the AI toward professional results: "highly detailed," "8K resolution," "professional photography," "award-winning," "trending on ArtStation," or "magazine quality." Experiment with Lighting and Perspective

Lighting transforms images dramatically. Specify "golden hour lighting," "dramatic shadows," "soft diffused light," "neon lighting," or "studio lighting setup" to control mood and atmosphere.
