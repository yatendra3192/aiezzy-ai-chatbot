Tips for Perfect Word to PDF Conversion

Expert recommendations to ensure flawless conversions every time

1

Prepare Your Word Document Before Converting

Review your Word document before conversion to catch formatting issues. Use Print Preview in Word to see how pages will appear in the final PDF. Check for orphan/widow lines, unwanted page breaks, and inconsistent spacing. Ensure all images are properly placed and not distorted. Verify that headers and footers display correctly across all pages. This five-minute review prevents surprises in the final PDF and saves time re-converting.

2

Use Standard Fonts for Maximum Compatibility

While AIezzy embeds all fonts, using standard fonts (Arial, Times New Roman, Calibri, Helvetica) ensures the best results. Exotic or rare fonts sometimes have embedding restrictions that can cause issues. If you must use special fonts, test conversion with a sample page first. For documents destined for official submission (job applications, government forms), standard fonts are generally preferred and reduce file size.

3

Optimize Images Before Adding to Word

Compress images in Word before converting to keep PDF file sizes manageable. In Word, select an image → Picture Format → Compress Pictures → choose appropriate resolution. For web use, 150 DPI is sufficient; for printing, use 300 DPI. This creates smaller PDFs that are easier to email and upload while maintaining visual quality. Large PDFs (over 10MB) can be problematic for email attachments and some submission portals.

4

Handle Special Document Elements Carefully

If your document contains forms with fillable fields, these convert to static PDF content by default. For interactive PDFs, you'll need specialized PDF creation software. Embedded videos and audio don't transfer to PDF—consider providing links instead. Track Changes and Comments in Word should be accepted/deleted before conversion, as they may not display in PDFs as expected. Convert the final, clean version of your document.

5

Verify the Converted PDF

Always download and review your PDF before sharing it. Open it in a PDF reader to check that all pages converted correctly, images appear as expected, and text is selectable (indicating proper conversion, not image-based PDF). Test any hyperlinks to ensure they work. Zoom in to verify text sharpness and image quality. For critical documents (job applications, contracts, official submissions), this verification step is essential. If anything looks wrong, adjust your Word document and reconvert—it's free and takes only seconds.
